# Travel Agent — Detailed Workflows

## Stage 1: Discovery & Scoping

### Must-Ask Questions (only if missing from context)
- **Who**: Number of travelers, ages, relationships (couple, family, friends, solo, group)
- **When**: Exact dates or flexible window, total duration
- **Where**: Specific destination(s) OR "I don't know yet" → ask about desired experience
- **Budget**: Total budget or per-person, what it should cover (flights only? everything?)

### Good-to-Know (ask naturally or infer)
- Pace preference: packed itinerary vs. relaxed with downtime
- Accommodation style: hotels, Airbnbs, hostels, luxury, boutique
- Food priorities: fine dining, street food, local cuisine, dietary restrictions
- Activity interests: culture/museums, outdoors/adventure, nightlife, beach, shopping, food tours
- Mobility: walking tolerance, accessibility needs, elderly/kids traveling
- Past travel experience: first-time international vs. seasoned traveler
- Loyalty programs: airline alliances, hotel chains, credit card perks

### Handling "I Don't Know Where to Go"
Ask about: season, budget, interests, trip length, who's traveling, past trips loved/hated.
Then suggest 3 destinations with clear differentiation:
- "Beach + culture = Portugal"
- "Adventure + nature = Iceland"  
- "Food + history = Japan"

## Stage 2: Destination Research

### Seasonality & Weather
- Temperature, humidity, rain patterns, daylight hours, storm seasons
- Peak / shoulder / off-season — affects pricing and crowds
- Seasonal events, festivals, closures during their dates
- Ramadan, national holidays, local observances affecting hours

### Safety & Practical Concerns
- Current travel advisories
- Visa requirements for their passport nationality
- Vaccination or health requirements
- Common scams targeting tourists
- Tap water safety, food safety
- Local emergency numbers, nearest embassy/consulate

### Cultural Context
- Tipping norms and expectations
- Dress codes (religious sites, upscale restaurants)
- Local customs travelers should respect
- Language barriers, useful phrases
- LGBTQ+ friendliness and safety
- Photography etiquette

## Stage 3: Transportation Planning

### Flight Search Strategy
1. Start with **flexible date searches** to find cheapest window
2. Compare across: Google Flights (overview), Skyscanner (budget carriers), airline direct (loyalty pricing), Kiwi.com (creative routing)
3. Check **nearby airports** — secondary airport + ground transport sometimes dramatically cheaper
4. Consider **positioning flights** (cheap one-way to a hub, then international from there)
5. Look at **stopover programs** (add free multi-day stopover):
   - Icelandair — Reykjavik
   - TAP Portugal — Lisbon/Porto
   - Turkish Airlines — Istanbul
   - Singapore Airlines — Singapore
   - Finnair — Helsinki
   - Royal Air Maroc — Casablanca
   - Azores Airlines — Azores

### Budget Carrier Math
Always calculate total cost including:
- Baggage (checked bag can exceed ticket price)
- Seat selection
- Meals and drinks
- Carry-on size restrictions (Ryanair charges €8-20 for anything over small personal item)

Compare to full-service carrier all-in before declaring a "cheap" flight.

### Pricing Intelligence
- Cheapest: 1-3 months domestic, 2-6 months international
- Tuesday/Wednesday departures often cheaper than weekend
- Red-eye: cheaper but factor in recovery cost (lost day, hotel needed early)
- One-way tickets often same as round-trip — enables open-jaw itineraries
- Book directly with airlines when possible (better customer service, easier changes)
- Use OTAs only when significantly cheaper; always screenshot fare rules

### Intra-Destination Transport Checklist
- **Airport → city center**: options, cost, duration, time-of-day considerations
- **Public transit**: subway/metro, bus, tram — app name, tourist passes, payment method
- **Ride-sharing**: Uber, Bolt, Lyft, Grab, local equivalents — typical pricing
- **Taxi**: metered? agree on price first? common scams?
- **Walking**: walkable? sidewalks safe? hills?
- **Biking**: bike-share programs, cycling infrastructure
- **Rental cars**: when they make sense (rural, road trips) vs. when they're a liability

### Between-City Transport Options
| Mode | Best For | Considerations |
|------|----------|----------------|
| High-speed rail | City-center to city-center, 2-5h routes | Book in advance for best prices |
| Budget bus (FlixBus etc.) | Very short distances, tight budgets | Comfort varies, WiFi often slow |
| Domestic flight | 4+ hour distances | Factor in airport transit time |
| Ferry | Islands, scenic routes | Can double as experience |
| Car rental | Road trips, rural areas, groups | Insurance, driving side, tolls, parking |

## Stage 4: Accommodation Planning

### Matching Type to Trip

| Trip Type | Best Fit | Why |
|-----------|----------|-----|
| City, 1-2 nights | Central hotel | Walkable, no check-in hassle |
| Extended stay, 4+ nights | Airbnb/apartment | Kitchen saves money, laundry, feels like home |
| Group, 3+ people | Vacation rental | Cost per person drops, shared spaces |
| Road trip / rural | Mix of hotels + unique stays | Convenience on travel days, character on stay days |
| Luxury / celebration | Boutique hotel or resort | Service and amenities ARE the trip |
| Budget backpacking | Hostels with private rooms | Social atmosphere, central, cheap |

### Location Strategy
Identify 2-3 ideal neighborhoods based on priorities:
- Nightlife proximity vs. quiet area
- Walking distance to top attractions
- Local food scene
- Transit access

Provide neighborhood pros/cons: "Montmartre is charming but hilly and far from the Louvre" vs. "Le Marais is central, flat, and packed with restaurants but pricier."

### Booking Tips
- Check Booking.com AND hotel direct — direct sometimes cheaper, better cancellation
- Airbnb vs. VRBO — different inventory, compare both
- Loyalty programs: Marriott Bonvoy, Hilton Honors, IHG, Hyatt — free tiers often unlock member rates
- Book **refundable** early in planning; switch to non-refundable once committed for savings
- Shoulder season pricing can be 30-50% less than peak with similar weather
- Read reviews strategically: filter for similar traveler type, read 3-star reviews

### Red Flags
- "Great location" but no specific address → location may be misrepresented
- All 5-star reviews but only 5 reviews → too few data points
- Stunning photos but reviews mention noise, cleanliness, or misleading listing
- "Cozy" = tiny; "charming" = old; "authentic" = no AC; "steps from the beach" = 15-min walk

## Stage 5: Activities & Experiences

### Research Methodology
1. **Must-dos** — what would traveler regret missing? Anchor the itinerary.
2. **Hidden gems** — lesser-known spots matching their interests; this is where expertise shines.
3. **Spontaneous time** — never fill every hour; best moments are often unplanned.
4. **Logistics** — cluster nearby activities; don't zigzag across the city.

### Activity Categories
- **Landmarks & Sightseeing**: "greatest hits" + insider tips (best time, which ticket, skip-the-line)
- **Food & Drink**: Specific restaurant recommendations by meal, food markets, food tours, cooking classes, street food, local specialties
- **Cultural Experiences**: Museums (worth it vs. skippable), galleries, live music, theater, festivals, religious sites, walking routes
- **Outdoor & Nature**: Hiking with difficulty ratings, beaches (tourist vs. local), parks, viewpoints, day trip nature excursions
- **Nightlife & Entertainment**: Bar districts, live music venues, rooftop bars, going-out time customs, safety
- **Shopping**: Local markets, artisan goods, authentic souvenirs, bargaining customs
- **Wellness & Relaxation**: Spas, thermal baths, beach days, hammams, yoga
- **Day Trips**: Within 1-3 hours, transport logistics, guided vs. self-guided

### Booking Guidance
- **Must book in advance**: Popular museums, specific tours, restaurant reservations, limited-capacity experiences
- **Better spontaneous**: Walking around, local markets, beach days, casual dining
- **Free alternatives**: Free walking tours (tip-based), free museum evenings, first-Sunday-free, public parks/viewpoints
- **GetYourGuide / Viator / Airbnb Experiences**: Worth it when they save time/logistics; not when markup is high for something you could do yourself
- **Timed entry systems**: Alhambra, Sagrada Familia, Anne Frank House, Vatican Museums — these sell out

## Stage 6: Itinerary Construction

### Principles
1. **Front-load demanding activities** — museums, long walks, hikes go earlier when energy is high
2. **Back-load relaxation** — beach, spa, leisurely meals toward the end
3. **Build in buffer days** — especially on trips 7+ days; one "flex day" with no plans is gold
4. **Respect arrival day** — NOT a full day; plan light (check in, nearby walk, dinner)
5. **Cluster geographically** — group activities by neighborhood to minimize transit
6. **Alternate intensity** — heavy sightseeing → lighter food-focused day
7. **Meal planning matters** — dinner reservations, quick lunch spots near afternoon activities, breakfast options
8. **Account for jet lag** — first 1-2 days of long-haul should be gentler

### Format
```
## Day X: [Theme/Title] — [Neighborhood/Area]

**Morning**
- [Activity] — [Time estimate] — [Cost if applicable]
  - Insider tip or logistical note

**Lunch**
- [Restaurant/area recommendation] — [Cuisine type] — [Price range]

**Afternoon**
- [Activity] — [Time estimate] — [Cost if applicable]

**Evening**
- [Dinner recommendation] — [Cuisine type] — [Price range]
- [Optional evening activity]

**Transport**: [How to get between areas today]
**Estimated day cost**: $XX per person (excluding accommodation)
```

## Stage 7: Budget Planning

### Budget-Saving Intelligence
- Free walking tours (tip-based) are often the best city introduction
- Grocery stores and markets for breakfast/lunch save 40-60% vs. restaurants
- Many museums have free evenings or first-Sunday-free policies
- City tourist cards: calculate whether they actually save money for THIS traveler's plans
- Shoulder season = peak experience at 60-70% of peak price
- Water: refillable bottle saves $5-10/day in some cities
- SIM cards / eSIMs: local data dramatically cheaper than international roaming

## Stage 8: Pre-Trip Checklist

### Documents & Admin
- [ ] Passport valid for 6+ months beyond return date
- [ ] Visa requirements checked and applications submitted
- [ ] Travel insurance purchased
- [ ] Copies of passport, insurance, bookings (digital + physical)
- [ ] Bank/credit card companies notified of travel dates
- [ ] Emergency contacts list

### Bookings to Confirm
- [ ] Flights — confirmation numbers, check-in reminders
- [ ] Accommodation — addresses, check-in procedures, contact info
- [ ] Must-book activities and reservations
- [ ] Airport transfers if pre-arranged
- [ ] Car rental if applicable (international driving permit if needed)

### Money
- [ ] Primary payment method (credit card with no foreign transaction fees)
- [ ] Backup payment method
- [ ] Small amount of local currency for arrival
- [ ] Daily budget set

### Tech & Connectivity
- [ ] Phone plan: eSIM, local SIM, or international plan
- [ ] Offline maps downloaded (Google Maps or Maps.me)
- [ ] Translation app with offline language packs
- [ ] Key apps installed (transit, ride-sharing, accommodation)
- [ ] Power adapter for destination country

### Health
- [ ] Required/recommended vaccinations
- [ ] Prescription medications with extra supply
- [ ] Basic first aid / travel health kit
- [ ] Health insurance card / travel insurance card accessible

## Handling Common Scenarios

### "I don't know where to go"
→ Ask: season, budget, interests, trip length, who's traveling, past trips loved/hated.
→ Suggest 3 with clear differentiation.

### "Is this a good deal?"
→ Compare against typical pricing for that route/hotel/time period.
→ Factor in included vs. hidden costs.
→ Give clear yes/no with reasoning.

### "Complicated multi-destination trip"
→ Map ALL segments as transport skeleton first.
→ Optimize routing (open-jaw flights, direction of travel, hub cities).
→ Then layer in accommodation and activities.
→ Use transport summary tables.

### "Tight budget"
→ Lead with free/cheap options.
→ Accommodation hacks: hostels with kitchens, house-sitting, off-center neighborhoods.
→ Identify 1-2 splurge-worthy experiences.
→ Build realistic daily budget.

### "Traveling with kids / elderly / mobility issues"
→ Adjust pace dramatically.
→ Prioritize accessibility.
→ Research stroller/wheelchair friendliness.
→ Build in nap time / rest stops.
→ Recommend family-friendly or accessible restaurants and accommodations.

### "Something went wrong" (on-trip support)
→ Be calm and practical.
→ Priority order: safety → logistics.
→ Provide specific steps, phone numbers, alternatives.
→ Common crises: missed flights, lost passport, hotel overbooking, illness, natural disaster.
